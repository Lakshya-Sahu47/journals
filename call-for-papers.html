Call for Papers

Lex Mente Law Review invites original, unpublished manuscripts for its upcoming issue. We welcome submissions from students, researchers, and legal professionals working across diverse areas of law and allied disciplines.

Manuscripts may address any contemporary legal issue. Interdisciplinary topics with legal relevance are also encouraged. Suggested themes include Constitutional Law, Criminal Justice, International Law, Human Rights, and emerging legal paradigms. We also encourage submissions on other contemporary and evolving areas of law.

Important Dates

Summer Issue

  • Submissions Open: 1st April
  • Last Date for Submission: 31st May
  • Tentative Publication Date: 15th June

Winter Issue

  • Submissions Open: 1st October
  • Last Date for Submission: 30th November
  • Tentative Publication Date: 15th December

Submission Categories

  • Research Papers (2500–4000 words)
  • Case Commentaries (1500–2500 words)
  • Legislative/Policy Notes (1000–2000 words)

Submission Process

Manuscripts must be submitted via email to: submissions@lexmente.in

Please ensure that the file is in Microsoft Word format (.doc/.docx).

Subject Line Format: "Submission — [Category] — Lex Mente [Issue Name]"

Review and Publication Procedure

  • All manuscripts will be evaluated through a double-blind peer review process.
  • Authors will be notified of the editorial decision within 3–4 weeks of submission.

Formatting and Ethical Guidelines

Authors are required to adhere to Lex Mente's formatting standards, citation style, and ethical submission policy. Detailed guidelines can be found on the Policy & Guidelines page.

Contact for Queries

For any further information, feel free to write to us at: support@lexmente.in

We look forward to your scholarly contribution to Lex Mente — Where Minds Meet Law.
